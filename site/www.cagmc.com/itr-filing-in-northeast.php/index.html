Simplifying ITR Filing in Northeast India: A Step-by-Step Guide

The Northeastern states of India, comprising Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura, boast unique cultural diversity, stunning landscapes, and a growing economy. With an increasing number of individuals and businesses in the region contributing to the nation’s progress, it is essential for residents of Northeast India to be well-informed about ITR filing procedures and tax regulations. What is Income Tax Return?

An Income Tax Return is a formal statement that taxpayers must submit to the tax authorities, declaring their total income earned during a specific financial year, along with the eligible deductions and exemptions. The tax authorities use this information to calculate the taxpayer’s liability, i.e., the amount of tax they owe or, in some cases, are entitled to receive as a refund. The process of filing an ITR is typically an annual obligation for individual taxpayers, while businesses may have different tax-filing requirements.
ITR or Income Tax Return is an annual document that taxpayers in India have to file. It is a declaration of income earned by an individual, Hindu Undivided Family (HUF), company, Association of Persons (AoP), an association of individuals, etc. for a particular financial year. All taxpayers are required to file an ITR if their annual income exceeds the taxable limit prescribed by the Government of India. Filing ITR helps the government to accurately track and assess the tax liability of individuals and companies.

Types of ITR Forms

The type of ITR form you need to file depends on your income source and category. Here are the commonly used ITR forms:

  • ITR-1 (Sahaj): For individuals having income from salary, one house property, other sources (like interest income), and having total income up to ₹50 lakhs.
  • ITR-2: For individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) not having income from profits and gains of business or profession.
  • ITR-3: For individuals and HUFs having income from profits and gains of business or profession.
  • ITR-4 (Sugam): For individuals, HUFs, and firms (other than Limited Liability Partnerships) having a presumptive income from business and profession.
  • ITR-5: For LLPs, Association of Persons (AOPs), Body of Individuals (BOIs), and Artificial Juridical Persons (AJPs).
  • ITR-6: For companies other than companies claiming exemption under Section 11 (income from property held for charitable or religious purposes).
  • ITR-7: For persons including companies required to furnish return under Section 139(4A), Section 139(4B), Section 139(4C), and Section 139(4D) (pertaining to charitable or religious trusts, political parties, institutions, etc.).

Income Tax Return filing in Northeast

Filing income tax returns is an important civic duty for people and businesses throughout India, and Northeast is no different. Northeast is significant in terms of its natural riches and rising economy, and as a result, only Income Tax Return filing in Northeast becomes critical.
Income tax is an important source of revenue for both the Centre and State governments. The taxes received through the filing of income tax returns go to the general revenue pool, which is then used for a variety of development projects and public welfare initiatives.
These funds are critical for the government to spend in infrastructure, healthcare, education, and other critical sectors, fostering economic growth and social improvement in the Northeast.
Individuals and businesses in the Northeast contribute to the stability and integrity of the tax system by filing tax returns accurately and on time. This not only helps the state’s economy, but it also builds trust between taxpayers and the government.
Several lending and credit organisations in the Northeast require income tax return documents as proof of income in order to handle loans, credit cards, and other financial services. Furthermore, filing an income tax return is required in order to benefit from government programmes, subsidies, and tax breaks aimed at boosting specific industries or improving social welfare

Why Should You File Your ITR?

The following are the reasons regarding why you should file your ITR?

  • Legal Obligation: One of the most compelling reasons to file your ITR is that it is a legal requirement in many countries. Tax laws mandate that individuals and entities meeting certain income thresholds must file their tax returns within the prescribed timelines. Failure to comply with this legal obligation can result in penalties, fines, and legal consequences.
  • Claiming Refunds: Filing your ITR becomes necessary if you have paid more taxes than your actual liability. By reporting all eligible deductions and exemptions, you can claim a refund of the excess taxes paid. This refund can be a significant financial relief and can only be availed through the ITR filing process.
  • Building Financial History: Regularly filing your ITR helps build a credible financial history. This history is often required when applying for loans, credit cards, or various financial transactions. Having a clean tax record can enhance your financial reputation and trustworthiness in the eyes of lenders and financial institutions.
  • Avoiding Penalties and Scrutiny: Timely filing of ITR reduces the likelihood of being subjected to tax scrutiny or audits by the tax authorities. If there are any discrepancies or errors in your tax return, the authorities may request explanations and supporting documentation. Filing your ITR diligently can minimize such risks.
  • Complying with Financial Institutions: Many financial institutions, such as banks and investment companies, often ask for ITRs as proof of income before providing certain services or facilities. Whether it's opening a savings account, investing in mutual funds, or applying for a loan, having a valid ITR can facilitate these processes.
  • Supporting Visa Applications: For individuals planning to travel or work abroad, a copy of their ITR are often required as part of the visa application process. Governments of other countries may request this information to assess the financial stability and compliance of the applicant.
  • Contributing to Nation-Building: Filing your ITR is a civic duty and an essential way to contribute to nation-building. Taxes collected by the government are utilized for various public welfare programs, infrastructure development, healthcare, education, and other essential services that benefit society as a whole.

Filing ITR in Northeast India

Residents of the Northeastern states, comprising Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura, follow the same ITR filing process as the rest of the country. The process involves the following steps:

  • 1

    Step 1: Gather Required Documents

    Before you start the ITR filing process, ensure you have the following documents handy:

    • Aadhar Card/PAN Card
    • Bank Statements and Form 16 from employer(s)
    • Proof of Investments (if applicable)
    • Details of Income from other sources (if any)
    • Details of previous year's ITR (if applicable)
    • Form-16: Provided by your employer, it summarizes your salary and tax deductions.
    • Form-16A: If you have earned income other than salary, like from fixed deposits or rent, you will receive this form.
  • 2

    Step 2: Selecting the Appropriate ITR Form

    Choose the correct ITR form that corresponds to your income source and nature of income. For most salaried individuals with no business income, ITR-1 (SAHAJ) is applicable. Other forms like ITR-2, ITR-3, ITR-4, etc., cater to specific income sources.

  • 3

    Step 3: Filling in the Details

    Once you've selected the correct ITR form, proceed to fill in the required details carefully. This includes personal information, income details, tax deductions, and exemptions availed during the financial year.

  • 4

    Step 4: Verifying Tax Computation

    Double-check all the details filled in the ITR form and ensure that your tax computation is accurate. Mistakes can lead to discrepancies in tax liability.

  • 5

    Step 5: E-Filing and Verification

    In Northeast India, where digital infrastructure might be limited, e-filing may present a challenge for some taxpayers. Seek assistance from tax professionals, local cyber cafes, or Common Service Centers (CSCs) set up by the government to facilitate the e-filing process.

After e-filing, the next step is verification. You can do this electronically through methods like Aadhar-based OTP, EVC (Electronic Verification Code), or by sending a signed physical copy of the ITR-V to the Centralized Processing Center (CPC) in Bengaluru within 120 days.

Punishment for late ITR filing under the Income Tax Act, 1961

The Income Tax Act, 1961, mandates this compliance to ensure an efficient tax collection system. Failure to file ITR on time can attract penalties as per the provisions of the Act.
As per the current tax laws, if an individual or entity files their ITR after the due date, they may be liable for two types of penalties:
Late Filing Fee: Under Section 234F, a penalty of up to Rs. 5,000 is levied for late filing. If the total income does not exceed Rs. 5 lakhs, the maximum penalty is Rs. 1,000. However, if the taxpayer’s income is below the taxable limit, the penalty is restricted to Rs. 1,000.
Interest on Tax Payable: In addition to the late filing fee, the taxpayer may also be required to pay interest under Section 234A. The interest is calculated at 1% per month (or part thereof) on the outstanding tax liability until the date of actual filing.
It is essential to note that these penalties are applicable irrespective of whether the taxpayer has any tax dues or receives a refund. Even if there is no tax liability, failure to file ITR on time can lead to financial consequences.

Key Points to Remember while ITR Filing in Northeast

The following are the key points to remember while ITR Filing in Northeast:

  • Deadline: The deadline for filing ITR is usually July 31st of the assessment year. However, it is advisable to file it well before the due date to avoid last-minute rush and potential technical glitches.
  • Tax Consultants: If you find the ITR filing process complex, consider seeking help from tax consultants who are well-versed with tax laws and regulations.
  • Digital Literacy Initiatives: Governments and non-governmental organizations in Northeast India have been working towards enhancing digital literacy. Stay updated with local initiatives that may assist you in the e-filing process.
  • Penalties: Filing ITR after the due date may attract penalties ranging from a few thousand rupees to more significant amounts, depending on the delay.
  • Be Honest and Accurate: Ensure that all information provided in the ITR is truthful and accurate. Non-compliance or providing false information may lead to legal consequences.
