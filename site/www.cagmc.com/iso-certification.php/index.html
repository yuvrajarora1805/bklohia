Introduction

ISO is an International Organization for Standardization that develops & publishes an international standard. ISO consists of representatives from different national standards bodies and aims at improvement in information security and protection of company assets. ISO has catalogued some internationally recognized standards like 9001, 22000, 14000 to bolster the exchange of goods at the international front. ISO certification improves the credibility of the business and helps to get more business. It must be noted that ISO is an autonomous, non-governmental global body with 164 national standards bodies as its members.

Series of the International Organization for Standardization reflect an array of standards for the Quality Management System rooted in the process model. ISO has an answer for all organizations which seek the way to enhance the quality of their goods & services and seamlessly meet the expectations of their customers. An ISO 9000 family is inclusive of renowned standards of ISO to address different aspects of quality management. There are a total of 14 quality management standards.
